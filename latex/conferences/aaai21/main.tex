\begin{abstract}
Our work is situated in the context of explainable artificial intelligence --- in particular explainable constraint satisfaction. An explanation in this setting is a sequence of `small' inference steps, where the cost of an inference step depends on the number and types of constraints used. This raises the need for algorithms that can compute unsatisfiable subsets of a logical specification that are optimal with respect to a certain cost criterion. 

We tackle this challenge by developing an algorithm for finding cost-optimal unsatifiable subsets that combines ideas  from
a hitting set dualization used for finding cardinality-minimal unsatisfiable subsets and a
hitting set dualization used for partial weighted MaxSAT. 

In the explanation-generation setting, many subsequent calls for cost-optimal unsatisfiable subsets will be performed on closely related theories. Inspired by this, we show how the hitting set dualization approach allows to reuse the knowledge stored in the sets-to-hit when solving many variations on the same problems. 

We evaluate the properties of our algorithms in the explanation-generation context, both in terms of the quality of the obtained explanation and in terms of the runtime.




\end{abstract}
